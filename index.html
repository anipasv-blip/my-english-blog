Critical Thinking & Media Literacy

Why Developers Should Always Verify What They Read Online

📅 April 7, 2025

In my opinion, one of the most important skills that a developer needs is not just coding — it is the ability to think critically about the information they consume every day.

We live in a world that is full of content which is not always reliable. Social media, blogs, and even technical forums can contain information that is biased, incomplete, or simply false. A developer who does not verify their sources can make serious mistakes — and so can any professional who works with data.

Critical thinking means asking the right questions: Who wrote this? What is the evidence? Is this source reliable? These are questions that every person who consumes media should ask, shouldn't they?

Media literacy is a skill that helps us evaluate information responsibly. I need to improve my ability to identify fake news and biased content. Neither do most of my classmates — we are all learning this skill together.

In conclusion, critical thinking and media literacy are skills that belong in every developer's toolbox — tools that help us grow not only as programmers, but as responsible digital citizens.
